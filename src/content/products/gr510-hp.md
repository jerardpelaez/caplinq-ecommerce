GR510HP Thermal Degradation Performance

Under inert nitrogen, **GR-510HP shows remarkable thermal stability**, with decomposition beginning at **T₅% &asymp; 475 &deg;C**. The **DTG peak (Tₘₐₓ)** at **419 &deg;C** marks the temperature of maximum mass-loss rate (&asymp; 0.00115 % / &deg;C). The total mass loss is limited to **8.13 wt %**, leaving a **high carbonaceous char yield of 91.87 % at 800 &deg;C**, indicating an extensively cross-linked epoxy&ndash;silica network and high filler content. The gradual, single-stage degradation profile with minimal DTG amplitude suggests a uniform decomposition mechanism dominated by the scission of epoxy backbone and filler-resin interface bonds without secondary oxidation.

In the oxidative environment, the degradation profile shifts slightly toward higher temperatures, with **T₅% = 540 &deg;C** and **T₁₀% = 668 &deg;C**, while the **Tₘₐₓ (DTG)** occurs at **428 &deg;C** with a slower mass-loss rate (&asymp; 0.000835 % / &deg;C). This counter-intuitive rise in onset temperature is characteristic of systems where **oxidative crosslinking or char consolidation** temporarily delays volatile evolution. However, total mass loss increases to **11.91 wt %**, and the **residual ash yield decreases to 88.09 %**, reflecting that oxidative cleavage ultimately consumes part of the carbonized structure. The result in the N2+O2 run is a more complete burnout of organic content/filler plus inorganic additives.
